Cloth that remembers the hands that made it

Vayan works with fourth-generation weavers, ajrakh printers, and bandhani artisans across Kutch — producing natural-dyed textiles for design houses, collectors, and homes that value the slow way.

The looms

Four living traditions

Ajrakhpur

Ajrakh Block Print

Sixteen stages of resist printing and river washing. Indigo, madder, and iron black — the same palette carried for four centuries.

Bhujodi

Kutchi Weaving

Extra-weft shawls and stoles on pit looms, woven in desi wool and kala cotton with motifs drawn from the Rann itself.

Bhuj

Bandhani Tie-Dye

Thousands of fingertip knots per dupatta. Each dot is tied by hand before the cloth ever meets the dye vat.

Nirona

Rogan & Embroidery

Castor-oil paint drawn in freehand thread-lines, alongside mirror-work embroidery from twelve village clusters.

Field to fold

How a Vayan textile is made

Fibre

Kala cotton & desi wool

Rain-fed indigenous cotton and local sheep wool, sourced within 100 km of the loom.

Dye

Vat & river

Indigo fermented in earthen vats; madder, pomegranate, and iron fixed by sun and river washing.

Loom

Woven by name

Every piece carries the weaver's name on its selvage tag. No anonymous cloth leaves the atelier.

Fold

Finished & documented

Each textile is photographed, numbered, and archived before it travels to you.
